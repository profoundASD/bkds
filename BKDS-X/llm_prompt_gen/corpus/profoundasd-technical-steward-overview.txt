[BKDS Engineering] Q: Why does the project recommend devices like the Kensington Orbit mouse or macro keypads over standard peripherals?
A: - Stationary stability reduces cursor drift from tremors/erratic movement. - Scroll ring supports gross-motor scrolling without precise on-screen scroll bars. - Ambidextrous symmetrical design supports hand switching. - Pairs well with 1-1-1 mapping due to large/simple buttons. - Reduces cognitive load by eliminating irrelevant keys from full keyboards. - Sends reliable collision-free signals intercepted by OS for scripted actions. - Provides tactile grounding via mechanical feedback to reduce repeated frantic presses. Single button can trigger multi-step routines (e.g., launch an app and set volume). Recommended to simplify the physical interface to match user motor skills rather than forcing adaptation to complex office peripherals.
